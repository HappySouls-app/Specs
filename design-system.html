01 — Principles

Five design values

Every visual decision — spacing, color, motion, type — should be measurable against these five principles. If a choice conflicts, it needs justification.

01

Literary

The interface should feel like a well-made book — weighted paper, considered typography, generous white space. Never a dashboard. Never a feed.

02

Warm

Cold blues and hard whites belong to productivity tools. HappySouls uses parchment, sienna, and amber — the palette of memory itself.

03

Quiet

Restraint is a feature. No hover animations that celebrate themselves, no color that shouts. The person's story is the loudest thing on the page.

04

Deliberate

Interactions move at a human pace. Transitions are slow enough to feel considered — they mirror the thoughtfulness the product asks of its users.

05

Timeless

This product will be used across decades of a person's life. The design must not feel dated in five years. No trends, no novelty — only endurance.

A palette of memory

06 — Motion

Slow is respectful

Motion in HappySouls is deliberate. Transitions take their time — not because they're indulgent, but because the product asks people to slow down. Hover over a card below to see the timing difference.

Fast — 150ms

ease-standard

Hover states, focus rings, button color changes. Micro-responses that should feel instant.

Default — 300ms

ease-standard

Card reveal, panel expand, dropdown open. The workhorse transition for most UI movement.

Slow — 500ms

ease-standard

Page transitions, major reveals, chapter navigation. Signals a meaningful context shift.

Deliberate — 700ms

ease-gentle

Session begin, biography publish, emotional milestone moments. Reserved for the important.

What motion is not

  • No bouncy, spring-physics animations
  • No celebration particles or confetti
  • No rotating loaders (use subtle pulse instead)
  • No animations that repeat on loop
  • No transitions under 100ms (feels glitchy)

Easing reference

Standard

cubic-bezier(0.4, 0.0, 0.2, 1)

Default for most transitions

cubic-bezier(0.0, 0.0, 0.2, 1)

Elements arriving on screen

07 — Voice & Tone

The product speaks quietly

The AI biographer has its own voice spec (see ai-biographer-characteristics.md). These guidelines cover the product interface: labels, prompts, empty states, error messages, and onboarding copy.

✓ Write like this

"Where would you like to begin today?"

Warm, open-ended, patient

✗ Not like this

"Let's get started! Pick a topic to unlock your memories!"

Exclamation marks, gamification language, dopamine tricks

✓ Write like this

"Session saved. Your words are safe here."

Calm, reassuring, specific about what happened

✗ Not like this

"Amazing! Your session has been auto-saved to the cloud ☁️"

False enthusiasm, jargon ("cloud"), emoji in serious moments

✓ Write like this

"Something went wrong. Your text is still here — try again when you're ready."

Honest about the error, reassuring about data safety

✗ Not like this

"Oops! Looks like something broke on our end 😅"

Deflective humor, emoji that trivialize a frustrating moment

Voice attributes

Warm Patient Unhurried Direct Honest Precise Gentle authority Dignified Never performative
