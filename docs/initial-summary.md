# Project "Legacy AI": Preliminary Specifications

## 1. Vision & Core Value Proposition

To provide a platform where any individual can preserve and transmit their life story through an interactive AI agent.

* **Human Impact:** Often has a therapeutic/psychological benefit via structured life review and meaning-making, but that is a side-effect — not the goal (and not a therapy product). Also: comfort for those with memory loss (Alzheimer’s) and a tangible "family heritage" for future generations.
* **The "Biographer" Philosophy:** Unlike standard LLMs, this agent is proactive. It doesn't just answer; it investigates, follows up on details, and identifies "gaps" in the narrative.

---

## 2. Key Features & Functionalities

### A. The Conversational Agent (The Interviewer)

* **Adaptive Questioning:** Starts with metadata (birth, parents) and moves into deep, open-ended questions.
* **Contextual Follow-up:** If a user mentions a brother, the IA asks for his name. If a user mentions a specific shop ("the tobacco shop with Dad"), the IA prompts for sensory details or specific anecdotes.
* **Recursive Memory:** The AI maintains a "Codex" of all previous sessions to avoid repetition and identify missing years or events.
* **Multi-Session Engagement:** Designed for the long term. The user can pause and return to specific topics later.
* **"Come Back Later" Threading:** The agent can explicitly park an anecdote or thread ("Do you want to come back to this another day?") and keep a lightweight backlog of open threads it can propose revisiting in future sessions.

### B. Data Structure & Management

* **The Codex:** A centralized repository of the user’s life facts.
* **Real-time Summarization:** Constant background processing to turn raw transcripts into structured summaries.
* **Timeline Indexing:** Automatic generation of a chronological life-map where every event is anchored to a date and reference.

### C. Output Formats (The "Deliverables")

The system isn't just a database; it’s a factory for various media:

* **Literary Styles:** 1st person ("I was born..."), 3rd person narration ("Chris was born..."), or Journal/Diary style.
* **Visual Assets:** * **Life Timeline:** A visual map of the user's history.
* **Visual Adaptations:** Conversion of stories into Comic Books (BD), Manga, or "Sims-style" avatars.
* **Video/Anime:** AI-generated video representations of memories (explicitly framed as interpretations/retellings, not ground-truth footage).


* **Physical Legacy:** Option to export a printable book with designated spaces for physical photos or integrated digital uploads.

## 3. User Experience (UX) Concepts

| Feature | Description |
| --- | --- |
| **Progress Visualization** | A "Life Map" or plan that fills in as the user speaks, providing immediate gratification and a sense of accomplishment. |
| **Voice-First Interaction** | Primary input via voice to capture tone and emotion, which also serves as an audio legacy for the family. |
| **Chapter-Based Release** | Users can "publish" and review specific chapters (e.g., "The Martinique Years") before the entire biography is finished. |
| **Multimedia Integration** | Potential for Facebook/Social Media API hooks to pull in existing photos and sync them with the narrative. |

## 4. Technical Parallels (Chris’s Developer Insight)

The project will follow a **Software Development Lifecycle (SDLC)** approach applied to literature:

1. **Global Framework:** Establishing the "skeleton" of the life story.
2. **Decomposition:** Breaking the life into "implementation phases" (chapters).
3. **Iterative Testing:** Refining chapters through feedback and "bug fixing" (fact-checking) until they "pass" the user's satisfaction.

---

## 5. Target Audience & Use Cases

* **The "Elderly/Ill":** For those wishing to leave a trace for children/grandchildren before they pass or lose their memory.
* **The "Ego-Driven":** High-achievers who want their "Life and Works" documented without hiring a human biographer.
* **The "Self-Reflective":** Individuals who want structured life review and perspective on their journey (therapeutic impact is common, but not the product goal).
* **The "Modern Youth":** Generations comfortable with AI who see this as a natural extension of digital memory (like an evolved Instagram).

---

## 6. Strategic Questions to Resolve

* **Photo Privacy:** How to handle users who are wary of giving AI access to their personal images.
* **Narrative Fidelity:** No implicit “gap-filling” or romanticization presented as fact. Stylized/romanticized versions (text or visual) can exist, but only as an explicit, clearly-labeled mode the user chooses.
* **The "End" Trigger:** Determining when a biography is "done" (User-defined vs. Life-defined).
