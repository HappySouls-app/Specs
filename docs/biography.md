# Biography

=> I find it interesting to include the reason why people start writing their autobiography. This could serve as the book's introduction. So capturing the *why*, the *when* (the period of their life or their age), the *how* (through the app, but how did they find it: a friend, an ad, personal research...), the *where* (where do they settle in to tell their story), and what they are feeling at that very moment (this can help those who don't know where to begin).

=> Have people recount culinary experiences with flavors, smells, and more. Not just culinary for the smells — but something that connects them emotionally and kinesthetically to a place, a moment, an event.
*(Memory reactivation option => go write in a place or around a dish that recalls a moment in life, allowing a more intense connection to the desired memory)*
*(Audio option => listen to a specific piece of music that allows you to dive back into a period of life you want to share / revisit)*

=> Ask people existential questions to prompt introspection or a review of a period? Such as: Who were you during that time? What do you hold onto as your best memory from it, and why? If you had to draw a life lesson from it — something for yourself and as wisdom to leave behind — what would it be?

=> Is it actually worthwhile to organize things for people? Or offer a guided bio vs. a free bio option.
Some will already have ideas and will want to write directly. Others will be intimidated and will need help getting organized. But being able to switch between the two is also great for those who can make some progress but not all the way.
So organize by period: childhood / adolescence / young adult / then by decade perhaps?
And create a summary for each period: What were you living through during that time? What is the best thing you can take away from it? If you had one piece of life advice to leave behind for parents or people at that age, what would it be? If there were trials to endure: what did you learn about yourself through them? Same for the life advice to leave behind, or to remember, to be kind to yourself.

=> Childhood section:
Where did you grow up? Describe your parents' apartment or house.
What were your parents like in terms of personality? Their qualities and flaws, and what positive and negative effect that had on home life. What did your parents do for work, but also in their lives outside of it: hobbies, friends, family — did they seem loved/appreciated? Their weekends, vacations? Were they religious, practicing? Did they have rules they held to?
How did they meet, etc... a bit of their lives too, in a few paragraphs. Especially if they are no longer living. This leaves memories for future generations.
*(All of this also sets a tone and a framework for understanding where people come from, and depending on the age / period at which the biography is written, it can bring comfort... generally lol). It also leaves a trace for the future of that era. And I find it wonderful to be able to retrace the atmosphere of different times through biographies. It gives an inside perspective from the people who lived it, rather than just referring to newspapers and history books, which only tell part of the truth...*
=> Do the same for each period, adapting to include friends / love interests / marriage etc... to be determined whether or not to include.
