# AI Biographer — Vision Backbone

## Core Idea

Build an AI collaborator that helps families preserve their stories before they’re lost.

Not a journaling app.  
Not a therapy replacement.  
Not digital immortality.

A structured, long-term system that helps a person articulate their life in their own words — and preserve it with dignity.

---

# The Core Promise

**You will be understood in your own words.**

Supporting lines:

- Before stories are lost, capture them.
- Your life, structured.
- Preserve your voice, not just your photos.
- Reduce narrative erosion.
- Capture your life as you would tell it.
- Continuity over fragmentation.
- A memory vault for families.
- The structured archive of a life.

---

# The Problem

Modern life fragments memory:

- Photos without context
- Social posts without continuity
- Stories told once and forgotten
- Family history collapsing across generations
- Personal memories fading and simplifying

Most lives disappear into fragments.

There is no structured system for ordinary people to preserve the narrative of their lives.

---

# The Product

An AI collaborator that:

- Conducts long-term structured life interviews
- Builds a chronological life map
- Detects gaps and asks follow-ups
- Preserves voice and nuance
- Tracks belief evolution over time
- Connects memories across decades
- Helps shape coherent chapters
- Produces a permanent, structured life archive

The “book” is not necessarily physical.
It is the structured, durable narrative of a life.

---

---

# The Mentality

This is not a growth-hack idea.
It is a craftsmanship idea.

Success metric is depth, not vanity metrics.

If 10,000 families are deeply impacted, it is meaningful.

Principles:

- Preserve voice over polish.
- Preserve contradiction over simplification.
- Preserve evolution over static identity.
- Ask better questions than a blank page.
- Never invent details as if they were remembered fact.
- No implicit romanticization: no silent “gloss” applied to the lived record.
- Romanticized / stylized retellings are allowed only when explicitly requested and must be clearly labeled as such (text or visual).
- Never flatten complexity.
- Always timestamp belief shifts.
- Always distinguish memory from interpretation.

The AI is not an archivist.
It is a collaborator.

It listens.
It probes.
It challenges gently.
It helps articulate.
It does not overwrite.
